News & Events

New Security Features Available As we have heard some members say that their accounts have been hacked and had withdrawals sent to other BTC addresses, we have decided to add the option for additional security features in your account.

After you login to your member's area, you will now see the option for "Security Settings" on the left side menu. On that page you will be able to change your account settings to detect any changes to the IP or browser you are using to login.

If someone tries to login from a browser or IP different than what you are normally using, an email will be sent to you with a link to verify your login.

Please note that you should only use this feature if you are using your correct email and have access to that email address.

Currently, this feature is only available on the English language version of our site, but will be added to all other pages soon. Oct-21-2017 04:16:56 AM
We have increased our minimum withdrawal amount from 0.0001 BTC to 0.0005 BTC. Due to increased miner fees and higher transactions fees when sending, we have been forced to increase our minimum withdrawal amount from 0.0001 BTC to 0.0005 BTC.

We understand that some members may be disapointed by this, but we are forced to do this as the transaction fees when sending a 0.0001 BTC transaction has become too high to be reasonable to for us to send such a small amount.

Please understand that this is for the long term stability and profitability of our system and for the benefit of all of our members. As always, all withdrawals will be sent instantly but the minimum amount to request a withdrawal has now been increased to 0.0005 BTC.

Thank you for your understanding in this matter. Oct-18-2017 01:05:41 AM
Update about Coins.PH deposits We have received feedback from some users that the reason deposits from Coins.ph is not being accepted by our system is because users are sending from PHP (Philippine Peso) wallet instead of BTC.

Our system will only recognize BTC deposits and not local currency deposits. Knowing this information, we have decided to update our policy to allow deposits using Coins.ph again, but users must send from their BTC wallet address on Coins.PH, and not their PHP currency account.

If you send anything besides BTC to us, our system will not recognize it as a deposit and our staff will not add your deposit. So please keep this in mind if you plan to use Coins.ph to deposit to our system. Oct-17-2017 07:54:41 AM
We are no longer accepting deposits from wallet "COINS.PH" Starting today we have made the decision to not accept any more deposits from wallet "Coins.PH" as these deposits are not being credited by our system. We don't know what the issue is, we only know that this issue is only affecting deposits from Coins.PH wallet.

If you have already deposited using Coins.PH before today, then please contact our support and they will collect your deposit information and it will be added manually within 24 hours.

If you deposit after today using Coins.PH, your deposit will not be accepted or added to our system either automatically or manually. We have also added a message on our deposit confirmation page about this as well as on our news page.

If you already have an existing deposit on our site and are withdrawing to Coins.PH, you will still continue receiving your withdrawals as usual, as only deposits are having a problem.

Again, this issue is only affecting users of Coins.PH wallet, and every other wallet user will be able to deposit and withdraw automatically as usual.
